Bruno Cuevas

I studied a Bsc in Biotechnology in UPM, then a master in Mathematical Modelling at UAB and now I work at CBGP as phD student.

I am currently interested in computational structural biology challenges:

  • Ligand difussion kinetics
  • Enhanced molecular mechanics sampling
  • Empiric energy methods
  • Electronic density models

Research

Ligand difussion process

Allergy is nowadays one of the most prevalent diseases in western societies, causing a large number of deaths per year and large costs to health systems. Meanwhile there is a vast knowledge about how to treat the symptoms of the disease, little is known about sensibilization causes.

Last year, out group reported for the first time the proofs of a sensibilization process driven by a ligand transfer process between lipid transport proteins and inmune system receptors.

The computational studies on protein-ligand interactions allow to understand the process driving sensibilization

Electron density models

In 1963 Feynman said Everything that living things do can be understood in terms of the jiggling and wiggling of atoms. Force field methods in structural biology have provided great results by considering biologically interesting systems as sets of atoms joined by springs.

However, many biological process can not be targeted using force field methods, and in many cases, the underlying simplification of the atomic structure can be misleading.

We are working in the construction of a electronic density model that can be applied at protein-scale systems without incurring in the huge computational costs of wave-function calculations.
