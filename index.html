Bruno Cuevas

BSc, MSc, and soon, PhD

Hi! Wellcome to my Brunosphere. I am a young scientist working on computational chemistry, where I am pushing state of the art models for electron density reproduction through machine learning. Besides, I actively collaborate with my colleages at Alislab, where I run molecular simulations to understand allergy and I am also working on epidemiological models for plant virus.

Just so you know: I am currently looking for a post-doc position

Research

My main research aims to provide a linear scaling method for electron density computation in large molecules. To do so, we replace the electron structure quantum-mechanics calculations by machine-learning estimations expanded in a linear set of functions. I specifically use neural-networks for this purpose. The result is a new methodology that is currently getting only a 2.5% absolute error in organic molecules, and a similar result in small proteins.

This is not over, given that chemistry depends of the smallest variations of the electron structure. I aim to keep working on the physical motivation of the learning process, as well as in the apllication of the model in combination with standard DFT.
